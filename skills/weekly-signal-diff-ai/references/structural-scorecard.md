# Structural Signal Scorecard

Use this to keep the weekly diff from becoming a news digest. Score candidate
signals before writing. Keep only signals with a clear structural change.

| Axis | High signal | Low signal |
|---|---|---|
| Constraint shift | Changes cost, latency, access, regulation, distribution, or defensibility | Product announcement with no changed constraint |
| Leverage shift | A company, buyer, regulator, developer, or supplier gains/loses power | Everyone's position stays basically the same |
| Dependency exposure | Reveals reliance on a model, cloud, chip, data source, channel, or regulator | No dependency changes |
| Economic impact | Changes pricing, margins, capex, inference cost, labor cost, or willingness to pay | Benchmark or funding news without economic consequence |
| User relevance | Affects the user's projects, tools, bets, or workflow choices | Broad AI news with no practical implication |
| Evidence quality | Primary source, direct announcement, filing, benchmark with method, or multiple corroborating reports | Unsourced rumor, vague commentary, or repeated press release |

## Keep Criteria

Keep a signal when at least one real constraint moved and the evidence is
strong enough to support a decision-grade claim. Funding rounds, product
launches, and benchmark screenshots need an added causal link before they count.
